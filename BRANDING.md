# TicketHawk Branding Guidelines

## Official Brand Name: **TicketHawk**

### Usage Rules

1. **Primary Brand Name**: Always use **TicketHawk** (not TixHawk) for:
   - Website header/navigation
   - Footer branding
   - Marketing copy and user-facing content
   - Modal dialogs and forms
   - Social proof/testimonials
   - Partner communications
   - Any customer-facing text

2. **Domain & SEO**: Use **TixHawk** (shortened) for:
   - Domain name: `tixhawk.com`
   - SEO metadata (page titles, meta descriptions)
   - Open Graph tags
   - Twitter cards
   - Schema.org structured data
   - URLs and technical references

### Rationale

- **TicketHawk** = Official brand name (professional, clear, memorable)
- **TixHawk** = Domain shorthand (concise, SEO-friendly, easy to type)

This dual approach gives us:
- Professional branding for users
- Concise domain for marketing
- Better keyword matching for "tix" searches
- Consistent brand recognition

### Examples

✅ **Correct Usage**
```html
<!-- User-facing branding -->
<h1>Join the TicketHawk waitlist</h1>
<footer>TicketHawk</footer>

<!-- SEO/Technical -->
<title>TixHawk - Concert Ticket Alerts</title>
<link rel="canonical" href="https://tixhawk.com/" />
```

❌ **Incorrect Usage**
```html
<!-- Don't mix them up -->
<h1>Join the TixHawk waitlist</h1> <!-- Wrong! -->
<title>TicketHawk - Concert Alerts</title> <!-- Less optimal for SEO -->
```
